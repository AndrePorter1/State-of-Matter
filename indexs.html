What is Matter?

Matter is anything that takes up space and has mass. It consists of tiny particles, such as ions, atoms, or molecules.

These particles come together to form different states of matter, which are determined by how they are arranged and how much energy they have.

Solid

A solid has a fixed shape and volume, with particles closely packed together. The particles vibrate but do not move freely, giving solids their hardness.

Examples: Ice, rocks, wood

Liquid

A liquid has no fixed shape but has a fixed volume. Its particles can move past one another, allowing it to flow and take the shape of its container.

Examples: Water, juice, milk

Gas

A gas has no fixed shape or volume, and its particles are spread far apart, moving quickly in all directions. This is why gases can expand or compress easily.

Examples: Oxygen, carbon dioxide, steam

Change of State

Matter can change between solid, liquid, and gas states through a process known as phase change.
